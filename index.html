The Problem

Your staff walk out of training with nothing to study.

In most restaurants, training happens on the floor — and stops the moment the shift ends. What does your team take home? Old binders. Printed menus. Handwritten notes. That's not training. That's hoping.

01

Hands-on only isn't enough

Floor training builds instinct but not knowledge. Without structured materials to study outside the building, learning stops when the shift ends.

02

Outdated materials everywhere

Old binders, printed menus, handwritten notes. Most restaurant training tools haven't evolved in decades — and your team can tell.

03

Turnover resets everything

Every time someone leaves, the knowledge walks out with them. A structured program gets new hires up to speed faster — and keeps them longer.
